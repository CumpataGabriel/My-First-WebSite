The universe (lat. universum) is the

totality of space and time,

of all forms of matter and energy.

While the size of the entire

Universe is unknown, the observable

universe can bemeasured and is

currently estimated to be 93

billion light-years in diameter.
A galaxy (the word comes from the

Greek γαλαξίας,galaxias, meaning

"milky circle", a reference to

the Milky Way) is a system with

mass, held together by gravitational

forces, consisting of an agglomeration

of stars, dust and interstellar gas as

well as ,but still unproven, invisible

dark matter and dark energy.
